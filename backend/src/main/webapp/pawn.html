Pawn

The Pawn is the front of your chess army, also making up the largest number of pieces at 8. They are also the weakest part of the army, being limited to moving 1 square forward(2 optionally at the start). They also can only take pieces in a diagonal square infront of them. This makes them quite situational but they can also be used to fill out the board and limit moves of your opponent.

If a pawn reaches the end of the pawn, they can also be promoted. Promotion means the chess piece takes on the abilities of another piece. Most players use a promotion to turn a pawn to a queen, as it is the most powerful piece but a player can have any amount. There is no limit to the amount of pieces a player can have promoted e.g. a player could theoretically have 9 Queens if all pawns get promoted to Queen.

Pawns can also perform an en passant. This occurs when a player moves their pawn 2 squares foward to avoid capture when if they moved 1 square forward, their opponent could of captured it. The opponent moves to the square behind the pawn, and the pawn is captured.

If the player does not perform this move immediately, they lose the option to do so it is advised to take out the piece unless you want to save your pawn from being taken out in the next turn.
